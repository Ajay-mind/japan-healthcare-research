Research Purpose

A clearer view of healthcare journeys for Japanese residents in India.

This initiative listens to the people who experience, coordinate, and deliver care so recurring challenges can be understood with context and respect.

Thousands of Japanese professionals and families live across India, especially around industrial and manufacturing regions. When medical needs arise, they may face unfamiliar hospital systems, language barriers, insurance procedures, and cultural differences.

The research captures these experiences through independent interviews with expatriates, HR and General Affairs teams, hospitals, clinics, medical coordinators, and insurance stakeholders.

Research Focus

Four lenses for understanding the patient experience.

01

Healthcare Access

How Japanese expatriates find and access hospitals, clinics, specialists, and emergency services.

02

Communication

Language, interpretation, expectation-setting, and cultural challenges during consultations.

03

Insurance Experience

Cashless treatment, reimbursement, documentation, approvals, and claim-related friction.

04

Patient Journey

The complete path from first concern through diagnosis, treatment, follow-up, and recovery support.

Privacy & Confidentiality

Participation in this research is entirely voluntary. Information shared during interviews will remain confidential and will only be used for research purposes. No personal, medical, or organizational information will be disclosed without prior consent.

This initiative is not affiliated with any healthcare provider, hospital, insurance company, or commercial organization. The purpose of this study is to better understand healthcare experiences and document insights from participants.
